EV-Bike Landing Page

Designing a sleek, futuristic product page for an electric scooter that highlights innovation, modern engineering, and user-first design.

UI Design Landing Page Product Design 2024

Project Overview

The EV-B website was designed to showcase a next-generation electric scooter with an emphasis on modern aesthetics and advanced performance. The objective was to craft a visually immersive interface that communicates innovation while making product information easy to understand for potential customers.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

3 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Team

Solo Project

Design Preview

The Challenge

Problem Statement

The client wanted an interactive, futuristic product page that immediately communicates the uniqueness of their electric scooter. The challenge was to balance clean minimalism with a premium tech-driven look, while presenting essential specifications—speed, range, and charge time—in a clear and appealing manner.

Research & Discovery

I conducted comprehensive research to understand the target audience and competitive landscape:

User Research

Through competitor analysis and user feedback, I identified key expectations for premium electric vehicle websites:

  • Strong visuals that highlight the product form
  • Clean, tech-inspired interface
  • Quick access to performance specifications
  • Clear CTAs like “Pre-Order”
  • Minimal distractions with focus on the product

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed leading electric bike brands to identify design patterns and opportunities for differentiation. This revealed that most competitors used cluttered layouts with too much information above the fold.

The Solution

Design Approach

Most EV websites rely on heavy motion graphics or overwhelming technical details. I identified an opportunity to create a smoother, more refined browsing experience with light gradients, simple typography, and an elegant content layout.

Key Design Features

I used a soft, futuristic gradient palette with teal and light blues to convey cleanliness and innovation. The scooter is placed front and center, paired with large typography and subtle highlights to maintain a premium look. Strategic spacing and minimalistic UI elements elevate the overall product presentation.

  • Hero Section: Bold, futuristic hero with the headline “Let's Ride the Future” and a full-focus product render.
  • Interactive Elements: Floating specification cards showing speed, battery range, and charging time.
  • Information Architecture: Smooth horizontal navigation with a clean top bar and a vertical concept label sidebar.
  • Call-to-Actions: High-visibility “Pre-Order” button placed directly below the hero text.
  • Mobile Optimization: Soft blue-teal gradients and centered product placement to create modern, premium symmetry.

Design Process

1. Wireframing

Developed wireframes focusing on a hero-first layout where the scooter becomes the primary visual anchor. Ensured clear flow between product details and navigation.

2. Visual Design

Refined the aesthetic with modern gradients, soft shadows, and large typography. The design language focuses on simplicity, clarity, and futuristic appeal.

3. Prototyping

Created an interactive prototype to test transitions, pricing visibility, and CTA placement. Ensured effortless navigation between product pages.

Results & Impact

The redesigned landing page delivered significant improvements in key metrics:

45%

60%

35%
Strong user feedback praising the futuristic and premium appearance

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important UX principles:

  • Less is more - a clean, focused design outperforms cluttered layouts
  • User research is essential for creating effective designs
  • Micro-interactions and animations enhance user engagement when used thoughtfully
  • Mobile-first design ensures accessibility across all devices
